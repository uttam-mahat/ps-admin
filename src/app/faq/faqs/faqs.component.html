FAQs
1: Just what is termite?

Termites are small social insect with distinct pale yellow, whitish with dark lines to brownish black colour and primarily feed on wood materials. There are various species of termites found in our ecosystem. Termites secrete a special substance called pheromone which is light yellow to brown in colour with certain viscosity. Termite builds up colonies in wooden structures, ground floor of buildings and severe infestation occurs in those structures. Termite has different castes like king, queen, soldiers, workers etc. the life cycle of termite is egg, nymphs and adults. Termite is a heavy feeder and feeds gregariously upon damp wood materials and other cellulosic substances.

2: Why are termites different than other insects?

Termites are well known social insects with its distinct caste system to work out on colony and affected zones. Termite secretes a special alarm pheromone for its communication within the group for searching food, nesting and caring of eggs. The termite proceeds in a two way lining with series of messages. Termites also feeds on ants and sometimes ants also feed on termites. Sometimes termite feeds on dead termites. When termites bite a person, it secretes a chemical and soon dies.

3: What do termites feed on?

Termites primarily feed on cellulosic wood materials especially damp wood, organic matters, papers, left over wood, bark of trees and other leaf litters, stored grains, leathery materials, low grade OPC and PPC cements, stones etc. Termites cannot feed upon the crux of the wood materials.
